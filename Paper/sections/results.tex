\section{Results}

Table~\ref{tab:firm-size-wage-premium-regressions} reports the baseline
firm-size wage premium estimates. Figure~\ref{fig:firm-size-wage-premium-over-time}
extends the full specification by interacting firm-size categories with year
dummies, keeping solo workers as the omitted category in each year. Thus, each
coefficient measures the wage premium for a given firm-size category in a given
year, conditional on gender, education, formality, and sector-year fixed effects.
The estimates show that the firm-size premium is positive throughout the sample
period, with the largest categories displaying both higher point estimates and
wider confidence intervals. Table~\ref{tab:firm-size-premium-trend-test}
formally tests whether each premium increased over time by interacting firm-size
categories with a linear year trend. Figure~\ref{fig:firm-size-premium-trend-test}
plots those trend coefficients and their confidence intervals. The estimated
trend coefficients are positive for all firm-size categories, but the one-sided
tests do not reject a zero trend at the 5 percent level.
