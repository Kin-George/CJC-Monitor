\title{Firm Size, Wages, and Labor Misallocation in Colombia}
\author{Oliver Pardo and Jorge Orozco}
\date{\today}

\begin{document}

\maketitle

\begin{abstract}
This paper documents the firm-size wage premium in Colombia using harmonized microdata from the Gran Encuesta Integrada de Hogares for 2008--2019 and 2021--2025. We show that larger firms pay substantially higher real hourly wages than solo workers and very small firms. A large share of the raw gradient is associated with observable differences in worker composition, formality, and
sector-year conditions. However, the premium remains positive and statistically significant after controlling for gender, education, formality, and sector-year fixed effects. In the full specification, workers in firms with 101 or more
employees earn about 35 percent more than otherwise comparable solo workers. This persistent conditional premium does not by itself prove misallocation, but it is consistent with labor-market frictions that limit the movement of workers toward
larger, potentially more productive firms. 
\end{abstract}
