\title{Firm Size, Wages, and Labor Misallocation in Colombia}
\author{Centro Javeriano de Competitividad}
\date{\today}

\begin{document}

\maketitle

\begin{abstract}
This paper documents the firm-size wage premium in Colombia using harmonized
microdata from the Gran Encuesta Integrada de Hogares for 2008--2019 and
2021--2025. We show that larger firms pay substantially higher real hourly wages
than solo workers and very small firms, but that much of the raw gradient is
associated with observable differences in worker composition, formality, and
sector-year conditions. In the full specification, workers in firms with 101 or
more employees earn about 35 percent more than otherwise comparable solo
workers. The premium is positive and statistically significant across the firm
size distribution, while evidence of an increasing trend over time is positive
but statistically imprecise. The results connect wage inequality with broader
questions of firm heterogeneity, informality, efficiency, and labor
misallocation in Colombia.
\end{abstract}
